Capacity Analysis: Ontario

Researchers from the COVID-MC collaborative (link) have developed a compartment model (link) to predict the time Ontario ICU resources will be depleted under undesirable (Italy) and better case (South Korea) scenarios. They explore scenarios varying the rate of growth of patients in the community/emergency department and ICU surge capacity. The effects of social distancing are modeled by decreasing the growth rate after 14 days.

Testing Analysis: Ontario

A casual glance at positive tests in Ontario, suggests the curve is relatively flat. Doubling time (not shown) has been decreasing. But a deeper analysis shows that delays in test results are piling up producing an artificial decrease in # of confirmed cases. Approximately 25% of cases are unreported; in other words, assuming a similar positive test rate (which is a conservative estimate given tightening testing criteria).

We also know that Ontario is not testing all patients with flu like symptoms (criteria link...Ontario.ca). Even with rigorous testing, we know there are approximately 10 symptomatic cases for each (Wuhan) positive test. Ontarioâ€™s ratio is likely even higher.
